Biography about Neil Armstrong

Childhood biography:

Neil Alden Armstrong had an airplane-loving childhood. He was born on August 5, 1930. He was the eldest of three children of Stephen and Viola Engel Armstrong. He thought that airplanes were cool when he took his first airplane ride. He started flying lessons when he turned 14, and on his 16th birthday, he was issued a pilot’s license before he got a car drivers’ one. Then, he built a small wind tunnel and performed experiments by using model planes he made by himself.

Older biography:

When Neil Armstrong became older, he joined the U.S. NAVY because he did not have enough money. When he joined the U.S. NAVY, he fought in the Korean war. Armstrong entered Indiana’s famous Purdue University in 1947 with a U.S. NAVY scholarship. After collage, he was a test pilot. Then, he finally become NASA astronaut. In September 1962, he became America’s first nonmilitary astronaut. When it became Neil’s Apollo 11, he blasted off on July 16 at 9:32A.M. eastern Daylight Time (EDT), Armstrong, with astronauts Michal Collins and Edwin Alden, lifted off from the Kennedy Space Center in Florida.
